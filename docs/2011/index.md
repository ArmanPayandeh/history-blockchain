While not a formal academic paper, the Bitcointalk announcement thread serves as the "whitepaper" for Litecoin. In this document, Charlie Lee outlines the technical vision for a coin that would complement rather than compete with Bitcoin. The document details the implementation of **Scrypt**, a memory-intensive hashing algorithm designed to make mining accessible to everyday users with CPUs and GPUs, rather than specialized ASIC hardware.

The summary explains the "Silver to Gold" analogy: where Bitcoin is a slow, heavy store of value (gold), Litecoin is designed for faster, everyday transactions (silver). The document confirms the total supply of 84 million coins (4x Bitcoin) and a 2.5-minute block time (4x faster). This announcement catalyzed the "Altcoin" movement, showing developers how to responsibly fork and iterate on the original Bitcoin codebase.

**Source:** Historical Archives / The Giving Block

This documentation tracks the intersection of Bitcoin and WikiLeaks during the 2011 "Financial Blockade." When traditional payment processors (PayPal, Visa, MasterCard) froze WikiLeaks' accounts under political pressure, the organization turned to Bitcoin. This event was a "double-edged sword" for the early community: while it proved Bitcoin's utility as a censorship-resistant tool, it also brought intense scrutiny from global governments.

Satoshi famously warned against this "heat" in late 2010, fearing the project was too small to handle the pressure. The 2011 archives show that WikiLeaks eventually ignored this plea, marking the first time Bitcoin was used as a lifeline for an international organization facing state-level financial de-platforming.
